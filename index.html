Bail Applications

Select the type of bail application you need

Pre-Arrest Bail (Anticipatory Bail)

Section 498 CrPC - Applied before arrest is made, when you anticipate arrest but haven't been arrested yet.

When to use:
  • For protection against false accusations
  • When arrest is imminent but not yet happened
  • Prevents police custody entirely
  • Filed in court having jurisdiction where arrest is anticipated

Post-Arrest Bail (Regular Bail)

Section 497 CrPC - Applied after arrest has been made, when you or someone is already in police custody.

When to use:
  • When person is already arrested
  • To secure release from police/judicial custody
  • Different from pre-arrest bail procedures
  • Filed in court where case is registered or accused is detained
